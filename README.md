# Agentic AI Threat Taxonomy

## About This Project

This repository provides a public taxonomy of agentic AI threats for AI governance, cyber resilience, and assurance in critical infrastructure.

It is part of the public thought-leadership work of AiGovra™, a framework focused on Ethical Agentic AI Governance for Cyber Resilience in Critical Infrastructure.

## Why This Matters

Agentic AI systems are different from traditional AI systems because they may plan, act, use tools, access systems, process data, and influence operational workflows.

In critical infrastructure, this creates new risks related to governance, cybersecurity, human oversight, auditability, service continuity, and assurance evidence.

This taxonomy helps organizations, researchers, cybersecurity teams, governance leaders, and policymakers identify and discuss key agentic AI threat categories.

## Threat Categories

| No. | Threat Category | Short Description |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Autonomy Drift | AI behavior moves outside approved boundaries or intended purpose. |
| 2 | Tool Misuse | AI uses tools, APIs, systems, or privileges in unsafe or unauthorized ways. |
| 3 | Goal Misalignment | AI optimizes for outcomes that conflict with safety, ethics, policy, or resilience. |
| 4 | Prompt Injection | Malicious or manipulated input causes AI to ignore instructions or perform unsafe actions. |
| 5 | Unsafe Delegation | Tasks are delegated to AI without adequate oversight, approval, or control limits. |
| 6 | Adversarial Manipulation | Attackers manipulate AI inputs, models, data, workflows, or decision pathways. |
| 7 | Excessive Autonomy | AI is given more authority, access, or decision power than appropriate. |
| 8 | Opaque Decision-Making | AI actions or decisions cannot be explained, reviewed, challenged, or audited. |
| 9 | Data Exposure | AI accesses, processes, shares, generates, or leaks sensitive data in unsafe ways. |
| 10 | Operational Disruption | AI behavior causes interruption, degradation, or failure of critical services or workflows. |
